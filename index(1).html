    Elevator for a 1000 floor building

    If I were to design an elevator for a 1000 floor building, I would prioritize its user’s convenience. Since an elevator takes an average of 2sec to go up a floor and another 3-6 seconds for the door to stay open, it would take it a few minutes to go up a couple of floors and having a building with 1000 floor the users might find it uncomfortable staying inside that enclosed space. So if I would design an elevator for that kind of situation, I would probably make it a bit bigger. The usual elevator size usually has a measurement of approximately of 6-7ft wide 5-6ft deep (front to back) and around 8ft high, but I would make mine almost double of that size, around 12ft wide 9-10ft deep and with a height of 9ft. I would add a place for them to sit on the sides with comfortable cushions and provide calming elevator music to keep them entertained while inside. I would also include air purifier that would provide a good scent that would quickly eliminate bad odor inside the enclosed space. The building would have several of these elevators, 1 for VIP use, another for people with disabilities, the elderly and the pregnant, (which would be accompanied with an elevator staff in case of emergency and also to assist them), another for the building staff, 4 for resident use and 2 for visitor use. The keypad inside the elevator would be a digital touchscreen.

    But instead would only include numbers and a button for emergency, enter, delete, close and open (for the elevator doors). Upon reaching a desired floor the floor number would be mentioned in the elevator speaker and the floor number would appear on the elevator doors where everyone inside would be able to see clearly. The elevators would also have a card reader feature, where residents, staff and people in the building could use using their cards/id’s and they would just tap it and the elevator would suggest a floor number where the user currently resides or usually goes. The elevator would record the user’s usual floor that they entered so that the user would just simply click one of the floor numbers instead of typing it.

    PETAGRAM

    In the brainstorming of the project, we, the proponents discuss our common thing and we come up about the things that we care and what make us entertain in our free time. With this, we notice our common thing which is our affection and interest to our pets and other animals and from here we try to discuss what we want and that is too surf the net with only pets and animals in our feeds or walls. In here, the issue and the problem regarding shelters that beg for donations rise up and animal situation from the zoo, laboratories, shelters and streets were also notice. As we want to answer this problem, we thought of something where people’s attention can be caught easily, and that is through social media. However, social media is a wide platform so we think of a way to narrow it down and that is by restricting a social media for animals and pets that leads to PETAGRAM. Other solution we suggest is to make a simple website or platform where people can find shelters to donate or be a volunteer. Just like how grab car works, this platform is for finding a nearest shelter where they can send help. However, this idea will not work based on our observation. As a group, we observed that people will just surf or read an information if there is a video or picture included to the information. We also observed that people are more active when it comes to socializing, talking to another user or having someone new as a friend. This observation soon leads to the idea of the PETAGRAM.
